1. Problem Statement
Enterprise AI Orchestration for Heterogeneous Data Ecosystems
Modern enterprises store information across multiple databases, cloud platforms, document repositories, and business applications. Business users often need answers that require combining structured data (databases) with unstructured information (documents, PDFs, knowledge bases), but today's AI assistants typically operate in isolated environments.
Organizations face several key challenges:
Fragmented Data Landscape: Critical business information is distributed across multiple databases, file repositories, and cloud platforms, requiring users to manually search and correlate information.
Vendor Lock-in: Existing AI solutions are tightly coupled with specific LLMs, databases, or cloud providers, making migration expensive and limiting flexibility.
High AI Costs: Every request is often processed by premium LLMs regardless of complexity, increasing operational costs unnecessarily.
Lack of Intelligent Orchestration: Current AI assistants execute prompts but do not intelligently plan, route, or orchestrate tasks across enterprise systems.
Limited Explainability: Business users have little visibility into how answers were generated, which data sources were used, or why a particular AI model was selected.
Slow Decision Making: Employees spend significant time switching between analytics tools, document repositories, and enterprise systems to gather complete information.
The result is higher operational cost, reduced productivity, slower business decisions, and limited trust in AI-generated responses.
2. Solution Provided
Enterprise Intelligence Orchestrator (EIO)
Enterprise Intelligence Orchestrator (EIO) is an AI-native orchestration platform that enables enterprises to securely query and reason over structured and unstructured enterprise data through a single intelligent interface.
Unlike conventional AI chatbots, EIO acts as an AI Orchestrator that dynamically plans each request, selects the appropriate agents, identifies the required enterprise data sources, and chooses the most suitable LLM based on complexity, governance policies, latency, and cost.
Key capabilities include:
Database-Agnostic Architecture supporting multiple relational and analytical databases through pluggable connectors.
Storage-Agnostic Document Access enabling seamless integration with local storage, cloud object stores, and enterprise document repositories.
LLM-Agnostic Framework allowing organizations to switch between open-source, enterprise, or commercial models without application changes.
Intelligent Planner Agent that analyzes each request and determines the optimal execution strategy before invoking AI models.
Dynamic Model Routing to balance accuracy, governance, latency, and cost by selecting the most appropriate LLM for each task.
Multi-Agent Orchestration where specialized agents collaborate for metadata discovery, SQL generation, document retrieval, reasoning, and response synthesis.
Evidence-Based Responses that combine database results, enterprise documents, and AI reasoning into a single explainable answer.
Enterprise Governance with policy-driven routing, auditability, explainability, and configurable security controls.
The platform provides a scalable, extensible foundation for enterprise AI, enabling organizations to adopt new databases, storage systems, AI models, and agents without redesigning the application.

Future Enhancement – Enterprise Identity & Access Management

Design the architecture so that Enterprise Intelligence Orchestrator (EIO) can integrate with enterprise Identity Providers (IdPs) using industry-standard protocols such as OpenID Connect (OIDC), OAuth 2.0, or SAML.

The authenticated user's identity, roles, groups, and claims should be propagated to the Policy Engine and Planner Agent. Before accessing any database, document repository, or invoking RAG, the Policy Engine must authorize access based on RBAC and ABAC policies.

All data sources should support configurable data classification levels (Public, Internal, Confidential, Restricted). Unauthorized records or documents must be filtered before retrieval and before any LLM receives the context.

The architecture should be compatible with enterprise identity solutions such as Microsoft Entra ID, IBM Verify, Okta, Keycloak, and other standards-compliant Identity Providers without requiring application changes.

This capability is part of the enterprise roadmap and should be designed as a pluggable security layer, not implemented in the MVP.
